FAQ

Questions, answered.

Is Chess Journal really free?

Yes. Download it and play — record your games, import from Chess.com or Lichess, browse your library, edit positions, and switch themes, all free forever. A one-time $5 payment unlocks Stockfish's full depth, on-device AI commentary, the Classics and Theory libraries, all 5,037 puzzles, and the Assistive Coach.

Can I buy the $5 unlock right now?

Not yet through this site or the app — the checkout (LemonSqueezy on Windows/Linux, Google Play Billing on Android) is built and tested internally but the storefronts aren't public. Download and use the free tier today; this page and the in-app unlock screen will go live the moment checkout opens.

What exactly does the $5 unlock?

Stockfish presets beyond "Eco" (Fast, Balanced, Deep, Max), the whole-game analyzer and move-quality report, on-device AI commentary, 9,254 classic games, 11,336 theory games, 5,037 puzzles, the Assistive Coach, PDF export, and the Profile/Opening-stats dashboards. Every future update to any of it is included.

Does it require internet?

No, for both the free and unlocked features — engine analysis, AI commentary, the game library, puzzles, and theory all work offline. Only Chess.com/Lichess import, checkout, and update checks need internet.

What platforms are supported?

Windows (installer), Linux (AppImage), Android (APK today, Play Store listing coming) — free to play on all three, same $5 unlock everywhere. iOS/macOS are not currently available.

How does AI commentary work without the cloud?

Chess Journal downloads a small language model (Qwen2.5 1.5B or Gemma 3 1B) to your device once you've unlocked it. All inference runs locally via llama.cpp (desktop) or Transformers.js WASM (Android).

Is my data private?

Yes. Everything stays on your device, free tier or unlocked. No telemetry, no analytics, no cloud sync. Your games, your analysis, your data. See the Privacy Policy for the few network calls the app does make (imports, update checks, checkout).

Do I have to pay again for future updates?

No. Your one-time $5 unlock covers every update Chess Journal ever ships to the unlocked features — new puzzles, new models, all of it. One payment, no renewals.

Is Chess Journal open source?

No — Chess Journal is proprietary, closed-source software. See Terms & License for what the free tier and the $5 unlock each allow.

Have feedback or found a bug?

Bug reports and feature requests are always welcome — email sazardev@gmail.com and it'll directly shape what ships next.
